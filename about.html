The Artisan's Story

Meet Marcus Vale

Master puppeteer, sculptor, storyteller — and the hands that give life to things that shouldn't move.

Origin Story

The Man Behind
The Strings

Born in a small seaside town in Portugal, Marcus discovered puppetry at age seven when his grandmother fashioned a simple cloth rabbit for him. He spent the next fifteen years devouring every tradition he could find — Sicilian marionettes, Javanese shadow play, Punch & Judy, Czech Fantoccini.

After studying at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art in London and completing an apprenticeship with legendary Bunraku masters in Osaka, Marcus founded Puppeter — a one-person studio that grew into an internationally recognized force in contemporary puppet theater.

Today, he balances live performance work with television productions, brand character creation, and a passionate commitment to teaching the next generation of puppet artists.

Journey

Milestones

2004

First Professional Show

Debut performance at Lisbon's Teatro São Luiz — a 45-minute solo puppet show that sold out its entire run.

2009

Osaka Apprenticeship

Two-year immersion with Bunraku masters at the National Bunraku Theatre, fundamentally reshaping Marcus's approach to character and movement.

2013

BBC Commission

Lead puppet designer and performer for the acclaimed children's series 'Dreamweavers', reaching 4 million weekly viewers.

2018

Edinburgh Grand Prix

Awarded the Edinburgh Festival's Grand Prix for Best Theatrical Production for 'Shadow & Light — a one-person, twelve-character epic performance.

2024

The Puppeter Studio

Opening of a dedicated public-facing studio in East London — offering workshops, residencies, and a permanent exhibition of characters.
