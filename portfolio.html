Original Characters

The Cast

Mister Cinders

A melancholic jester built from ash-grey felt and vintage buttons. Hero of the 'Shadow & Light' performances. 48cm rod puppet.

The Copper Fox

Animatronic foam-latex fox with servo-driven eyes and cable mouth. Star of the BBC Dreamweavers series. Radio-controlled.

The Watcher

A two-meter shadow puppet cut from lambskin leather, based on Javanese Wayang tradition. Used in international touring shows.

Pelagic

An undulating ocean creature operated by six performers. Constructed from silk, cane, and translucent resin — 4 meters long.

The Making Of

Creation Process

01

Character Brief

Deep research into story, psychology, and cultural context. Character sketches and personality documents are produced first.

02

Maquette

A small-scale model is sculpted in oil-based clay to establish form, proportion, and character expression before full-size work begins.

03

Full Sculpt

The final sculpture is produced in the chosen medium — carved foam, cast resin, or built-up latex over an armature.

04

Mechanism

Internal rigging is designed and installed — strings, rods, cables, or servo systems depending on the puppet's performance requirements.

05

Finishing

Painting, costuming, hair, and final detailing bring the character to its visual completion. Each step photographed for reference.

06

Rehearsal

The puppeteer learns the character over weeks of rehearsal — finding its walk, voice, breath, and inner life.
